# Ask for Domain Rules Before Deriving Proxies

When a task implies an ordering or grouping that depends on a domain concept not visible in the data, don't substitute a geometric or structural proxy and present the result as derived — ask for the actual rule before computing. A plausible-looking proxy can be confirmed wrong after being applied; two clarifying questions up front would have caught it before any edits were made.

Especially relevant for business-logic domains (HR, project management) where ordering rules reflect policy or hierarchy, not geometry.
